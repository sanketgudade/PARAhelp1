Welcome to the Assistive Interface

For Caretakers

The Assistive Interface is designed to help individuals with limited mobility or communication difficulties express their needs and desires more easily. Below are some key points to understand and use this interface effectively:

  • Buttons: Each button represents a specific need or request. The icon on each button provides a visual cue for its function.
  • Interaction: When a button is clicked, the corresponding message will be displayed on the screen, and an alert will be sent to the specified phone number.
  • Sound Alert: A beep sound will play to grab attention when a button is pressed, ensuring that the request is noticed promptly.
  • Message Display: The message will appear in a scrolling marquee on the screen to ensure clarity and visibility.
  • Visual Indicators: The outline of the buttons rotates periodically to draw attention to different options.

For Users

This interface allows you to communicate your needs by simply pressing a button. Here's how it works:

  • Press a Button: Each button has an image and label that represent a different need or request, like "Food," "Toilet," or "Help."
  • What Happens: When you press a button, a message will appear on the screen and a sound will play to notify your caretaker.
  • Response Time: The interface is designed to respond quickly to your needs. After pressing a button, your caretaker will be alerted and will come to assist you.
  • Additional Buttons: There are also buttons for accessing popular websites and services, like Instagram and YouTube, for entertainment and information.
